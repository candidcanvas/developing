Module 1: Concept

Subject Research

In this introductory module, I, with my teammate Khant Nyar Lu, looked into different physical computing and 3D design practical applications as paths to pursue for a semester-long project. We explored a variety of concepts through relevant case studies and techniques for audiovisual design in live interactive and performance settings.

Path research

I looked into audiovisual design and projection mapping as my potential subject matters for the semester. My findings focused on recent case studies that employ techniques and concepts that I want to explore further. Audiovisual design and projection mapping are fairly niche in the design world, so I shifted from looking into trends to researching how these technologies are being used in the entertainment industry today.

Informed ideation

I connected with Khant, since he was also interested in learning TouchDesigner and pursuing audiovisual design with his own music. We decided to collaborate and fill in the gaps of our collective knowledge in music production and audiovisual design. After peer and prof feedback, we stepped back from creating a performance set/installation to instead focus on developing technical skills. Instead of worrying about the logistics of putting up a whole perfomance, our time was better spent refining our skills trhough smaller scale experiments and projects.

Project Concept

Over the course of this semester, we dove deeper into learning live audiovisual techniques using TouchDesigner and Ableton. We explored a variety of different interactions that can later be integrated into meaningful music experiences, such as live performances, installations, exhibitions, and personal projects. We aim to strengthen our technical skills and build confidence for later application on larger-scale projects.

Module 2: Prototype

Action Research

Khant and I conducted action research into and explored the other’s disciplines and strengths. We aim to first know the rules through understanding the boundaries and possibilities of using TouchDesigner and Ableton together before breaking them by diving into our own custom networks in the final module. While Khant was working on familiarizing himself with using TouchDesigner, I explored Ableton Live Suite, a music production software, and how to integrate it in TouchDesigner to use in real-time audiovisual development. We also took the opportunity to learn about hand tracking using the MediaPipe computer vision plugin for TouchDesigner, and how to use it to control tracks.

TouchDesigner x Ableton

MediaPipe hand tracking

Prototype and Working Concept

In understanding how the other person works, we plan to come together in the final module to combine our findings and complement each other’s strengths. Khant's activities surrounded TouchDesigner and the integration of Ableton tracks, and my learnings on using MediaPipe for hand tracking controls. After gaining familiarity in each other's disciplines, we reconvened to talk about how we can move towards combining our findings for our visuals in the final module. There was overlap in our research concerning MediaPipe hand tracking controls, so we planned to our visuals around these controls, but he takes over the sound design and music production while I take care of the corresponding and reactive visuals.

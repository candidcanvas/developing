Special Topics Case Study

Research & Activity Documentation

Bre Velasco and Khant Nyar Lu

Introduction

My time with my teammate, Khant Nyar Lu, during this course was spent exploring the capabilities of generative art using TouchDesigner, learning about computer vision input to recognize hand tracking using the MediaPipe plugin, and the integration of Ableton Live Suite to control tracks in real-time. The confidence we’ve built and the technical skills we’ve learned helps provide a strong foundation that can go towards our interests and career goals in designing larger scale immersive, multi-sensory experiences.

Module 1: Concept

Subject Research

In this introductory module, I, with my teammate Khant Nyar Lu, looked into different physical computing and 3D design practical applications as paths to pursue for a semester-long project. We explored a variety of concepts through relevant case studies and techniques for audiovisual design in live interactive and performance settings.

Path research

I looked into audiovisual design and projection mapping as my potential subject matters for the semester. My findings focused on recent case studies that employ techniques and concepts that I want to explore further. Audiovisual design and projection mapping are fairly niche in the design world, so I shifted from looking into trends to researching how these technologies are being used in the entertainment industry today.

Informed ideation

I connected with Khant, since he was also interested in learning TouchDesigner and pursuing audiovisual design with his own music. We decided to collaborate and fill in the gaps of our collective knowledge in music production and audiovisual design. After peer and prof feedback, we stepped back from creating a performance set/installation to instead focus on developing technical skills. Instead of worrying about the logistics of putting up a whole perfomance, our time was better spent refining our skills trhough smaller scale experiments and projects.

Project Concept

Over the course of this semester, we dove deeper into learning live audiovisual techniques using TouchDesigner and Ableton. We explored a variety of different interactions that can later be integrated into meaningful music experiences, such as live performances, installations, exhibitions, and personal projects. We aim to strengthen our technical skills and build confidence for later application on larger-scale projects.

Module 2: Prototype

Action Research

Khant and I conducted action research into and explored the other’s disciplines and strengths. We aim to first know the rules through understanding the boundaries and possibilities of using TouchDesigner and Ableton together before breaking them by diving into our own custom networks in the final module. While Khant was working on familiarizing himself with using TouchDesigner, I explored Ableton Live Suite, a music production software, and how to integrate it in TouchDesigner to use in real-time audiovisual development. We also took the opportunity to learn about hand tracking using the MediaPipe computer vision plugin for TouchDesigner, and how to use it to control tracks.

Prototype and Working Concept

In understanding how the other person works, we plan to come together in the final module to combine our findings and complement each other’s strengths. Khant's activities surrounded TouchDesigner and the integration of Ableton tracks, and my learnings on using MediaPipe for hand tracking controls. After gaining familiarity in each other's disciplines, we reconvened to talk about how we can move towards combining our findings for our visuals in the final module. There was overlap in our research concerning MediaPipe hand tracking controls, so we planned to our visuals around these controls, but he takes over the sound design and music production while I take care of the corresponding and reactive visuals.

Module 3: Product

Development

This final module of the 3D Design Sandbox Special Topics course was the final production stage that fully utilized the culmination of our collective technical skills developed over the semester. Khant and I combined our findings and designed two live interactive audiovisual systems that combined our interests within the intersection of music and design.

Reactive audio

Responsive UI

Final Product

System A layers a series of camera effects and blob tracking filters with a reactive audio system to create a playful, interactive experience that allows users to create music in an unconventional manner. System B utilizes a gesture control system that routes to custom Ableton tracks, allowing performers to mix their voice in real-time.

Research Summation

Our first module was an open exploration of potential subject matters that we could potentially explore over the semester. It was then that I connected with Khant over our shared interests in incorporating music and learning more about audiovisual design through TouchDesigner. Once we settled on our topic, we pursued it further through informed ideation based on analyses of past and current case studies that also use TouchDesigner and explore how music and digital visuals can influence each other in a performance setting.

Our second module was dedicated to action research into the integration of TouchDesigner, Ableton, and MediaPipe into a single workflow. Khant and I took it as an opportunity to learn about each other’s disciplines in audio and music production and generative art and design in order to familiarize ourselves with the overall production process of our final products. During this module, I personally took the time to learn about the basics of audio production in Ableton and how to integrate it into TouchDesigner, as well as learning about how to use the MediaPipe plugin for its low-latency computer vision capabilities to use hand tracking input. My activities centred on using tracks as input to create visuals, input from TouchDesigner to create audio output, and demystifying MediaPipe and learning how to use it for a playful user experience.

Reflection

Over the course of this semester, Khant and I took a deep dive into live audiovisual design through the development of custom networks using TouchDesigner and Ableton. We focused specifically on developing our technical skills with these tools with the aim using them in later applications in larger-scale performances, such as concerts or clubs, or installation settings, such as museums or galleries. Our action research and production phases looked specifically into the integration of the MediaPipe computer vision plugin for TouchDesigner and how we can use hand tracking as an unconventional way of interacting with live tracks in the Ableton Live Suite. In exploring each other’s interests, Khant with generative art and I with audio production, we opened up a world of possibilities in the intersection of design and music.

Our learnings through this semester-long project set a strong foundation of technical skill that helped us grow more confident in our approaches to audiovisual design. I feel that we now have a more informed understanding of live, interactive audiovisuals that can be put towards our personal work and disciplines moving forward as we transition into our careers. These systems acted as the groundwork for later projects that we can explore, especially with Khant’s music production projects and me with my exploration of large-scale immersive experiences. Overall, I feel very proud and satisfied with the systems we’ve developed and I looked forward to what other projects we may take up in the future, within academic, professional, and personal projects.
